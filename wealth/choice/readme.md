# choice

## start with the basic truths
i. **choice is selecting among options.**  
   you can’t choose everything.

ii. **choice creates trade‑offs.**  
   picking one means giving up another.

iii. **choice shapes outcomes.**  
   decisions compound over time.

iv. **choice depends on constraints.**  
   time, information, and resources limit options.

---

## what is choice?

**choice** = **the act of selecting one option from many within constraints.**

it is:

• directional  
• consequential  
• limited  

---

## choice across key life resources

### time
- **choice use** = deliberate priorities  
- **choice loss** = drift and distraction  

### money
- **choice use** = intentional spending  
- **choice loss** = impulsive costs  

### skills
- **choice use** = focused practice  
- **choice loss** = scattered effort  

### knowledge
- **choice use** = seeking what matters  
- **choice loss** = chasing trivia  

### tools
- **choice use** = picking tools that fit the work  
- **choice loss** = tool overload  

### relationships
- **choice use** = investing in supportive bonds  
- **choice loss** = keeping harmful ties  

### networks
- **choice use** = building strong circles  
- **choice loss** = weak connections  

### energy
- **choice use** = choosing rest and recovery  
- **choice loss** = constant overextension  

### health
- **choice use** = daily habits that protect the body  
- **choice loss** = neglect and inconsistency  

### capital
- **choice use** = compounding the right resources  
- **choice loss** = wasting core assets

## a simple example

you have one hour:
- you decide to learn a skill  
- you skip scrolling  

now your choice creates growth.

## first principles summary

**choice = selecting an option within limits.**  
choose well, and outcomes improve.  
choose poorly, and costs compound.
