# persuasion.

## start with the basic truths
i. **people change when reasons feel relevant.**  
   logic alone is often insufficient.

ii. **credibility affects acceptance.**  
   trust in the source changes how messages land.

iii. **emotion and narrative move attention.**  
   people remember meaning before detail.

iv. **friction blocks action.**  
   even agreement fails if the next step is hard.

---

## what is persuasion?

**persuasion** = **the process of influencing beliefs, attitudes, or actions through communication and context rather than force.**

---

## persuasion breakdown

1. **audience**  
   goals, fears, incentives, and current beliefs.

2. **message**  
   claim, evidence, framing, and emotional relevance.

3. **messenger**  
   perceived expertise, trustworthiness, and alignment.

4. **channel and timing**  
   where and when the message is delivered.

5. **social proof and norms**  
   signals of what peers believe or do.

6. **action design**  
   clear, low-friction next steps that make behavior easy.

---

## a simple example

you want your team to adopt documentation:
- show lost time from repeated questions
- share a simple template
- ask for one small pilot habit

persuasion works when **evidence + trust + easy action** align.

---

## persuasion summary

**persuasion = ethically influencing choice through clear value, trusted delivery, and low-friction action.**
