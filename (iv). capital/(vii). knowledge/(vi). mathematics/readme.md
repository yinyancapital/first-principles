# mathematics.

## start with the basic truths
i. **patterns can be represented symbolically.**  
   symbols let us reason beyond immediate perception.

ii. **structure creates predictability.**  
   consistent rules allow reliable inference.

iii. **proof separates belief from certainty.**  
   claims need logical justification.

iv. **abstraction scales understanding.**  
   one formal model can explain many specific cases.

---

## what is mathematics?

**mathematics** = **the formal study of quantity, structure, change, and logic using definitions, symbols, and proofs.**

---

## mathematics breakdown

1. **arithmetic and number**  
   counting, operations, ratios, and magnitude.

2. **algebra and symbolic relations**  
   expressing unknowns and constraints in equations.

3. **geometry and spatial structure**  
   forms, distance, shape, and transformation.

4. **calculus and change**  
   rates, accumulation, and continuous systems.

5. **probability and statistics**  
   uncertainty, variation, and data-based inference.

6. **logic and proof**  
   valid reasoning and rigorous demonstration.

---

## a simple example

you track savings growth:
- deposits are discrete additions (arithmetic)
- interest is rate-based change over time (calculus)
- uncertainty in returns needs probability thinking

mathematics gives a model to **forecast, compare, and decide**.

---

## mathematics summary

**mathematics = a precise language for patterns, structure, and justified reasoning.**
