# reputation — first principles

## start with the basic truths
i. **people judge patterns of behavior.**  
   reputation forms from repeated actions.

ii. **trust is earned slowly.**  
   it grows through consistency.

iii. **trust is lost quickly.**  
   one breach can undo years.

iv. **reputation affects access.**  
   it opens or closes doors.

---

## what is reputation (first principles)?

**reputation** = **the shared belief about your reliability and character.**

it comes from:

• consistency  
• integrity  
• results  
• respect  

---

## reputation across key life resources

### time
- **reputation use** = reliability saves time  
- **reputation loss** = delays from doubt  

### money
- **reputation use** = better deals and terms  
- **reputation loss** = higher costs and barriers  

### skills
- **reputation use** = chances to apply skills  
- **reputation loss** = fewer opportunities  

### knowledge
- **reputation use** = being trusted for insight  
- **reputation loss** = ignored advice  

### tools
- **reputation use** = access to better tools  
- **reputation loss** = limited access  

### relationships
- **reputation use** = deeper trust and loyalty  
- **reputation loss** = distance and suspicion  

### networks
- **reputation use** = strong referrals  
- **reputation loss** = weak connections  

### energy
- **reputation use** = less friction and stress  
- **reputation loss** = constant defense  

### health
- **reputation use** = supportive care and accountability  
- **reputation loss** = isolation  

---

## a simple example

you deliver on promises:
- show up on time  
- finish what you start  

now people trust you with bigger work.

---

## first principles summary

**reputation = the shared belief about your reliability.**  
protect it, and opportunity grows.  
damage it, and options shrink.
