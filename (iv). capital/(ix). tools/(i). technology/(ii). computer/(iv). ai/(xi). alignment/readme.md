# alignment.

## start with the basic truths
i. **capability and intent are different.**
   a powerful model can still pursue the wrong objective.

ii. **human preferences are complex.**
   values are multi-dimensional and context dependent.

iii. **specification can be incomplete.**
   written instructions never capture every edge case.

iv. **safety requires ongoing control.**
   aligned behavior must hold across tasks and time.

---

## what is alignment?

**alignment** = **the degree to which an ai system's behavior matches intended human goals, values, and safety constraints.**

---

## how alignment is pursued

1. **define desired behavior**
   specify goals, constraints, and unacceptable outputs.

2. **train preference-sensitive models**
   use supervision and feedback to shape responses.

3. **evaluate across scenarios**
   test harmlessness, honesty, and robustness.

4. **add safeguards**
   apply policy filters, monitoring, and fallback controls.

5. **iterate with real-world feedback**
   update as failures and new contexts appear.

---

## why alignment matters

- it **reduces harmful or deceptive behavior**
- it **improves trust and reliability in deployment**
- it **bridges model objectives with human intent**
- it **is essential for safe scaling of capability**

---

## alignment summary

**alignment = model behavior kept in-bounds with intended human objectives.**
