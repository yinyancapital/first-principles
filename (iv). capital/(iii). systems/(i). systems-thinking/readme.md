# systems thinking.

## start with the basic truths
i. **events come from structures.**  
   repeated outcomes usually reflect system design.

ii. **parts interact through feedback.**  
   actions loop back to influence future behavior.

iii. **delays hide cause and effect.**  
   consequences often arrive later than actions.

iv. **local fixes can create global problems.**  
   optimizing one part can weaken the whole.

---

## what is systems thinking?

**systems thinking** = **a way of understanding problems by examining relationships, feedback loops, and underlying structures over time rather than isolated events.**

---

## systems thinking breakdown

1. **elements and boundaries**  
   identify components and what is inside/outside scope.

2. **interconnections**  
   map how parts influence one another.

3. **feedback loops**  
   reinforcing loops amplify; balancing loops stabilize.

4. **stocks and flows**  
   stocks accumulate; flows increase or decrease them.

5. **delays and nonlinearity**  
   effects may be delayed and disproportionate.

6. **leverage points**  
   small structural changes can produce large outcomes.

---

## a simple example

a team keeps missing deadlines:
- manager adds pressure (event-level fix)
- stress increases errors
- rework grows backlog
- delays worsen despite more pressure

systems thinking reframes this as a **feedback-loop problem**, not a motivation problem.

---

## systems thinking summary

**systems thinking = see structures and loops, then change the system where leverage is highest.**
