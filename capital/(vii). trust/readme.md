# trust — first principles

## start with the basic truths
i. **trust reduces friction.**  
   when people trust you, things move faster.

ii. **trust is earned by consistency.**  
   small reliable actions build it.

iii. **trust is fragile.**  
   one breach can reset progress.

iv. **trust compounds.**  
   over time it creates access and leverage.

---

## what is trust (first principles)?

**trust** = **confidence in someone’s reliability, integrity, and intent.**

it grows from:

• honesty  
• follow‑through  
• care  
• competence  

---

## trust across key life resources

### time
- **trust use** = fewer checks, faster decisions  
- **trust loss** = delays and verification  

### money
- **trust use** = better terms and access  
- **trust loss** = higher costs  

### skills
- **trust use** = bigger responsibilities  
- **trust loss** = limited roles  

### knowledge
- **trust use** = advice that is valued  
- **trust loss** = ignored insight  

### tools
- **trust use** = shared tools and access  
- **trust loss** = restricted access  

### relationships
- **trust use** = deeper connection and safety  
- **trust loss** = distance and doubt  

### networks
- **trust use** = strong referrals  
- **trust loss** = weak introductions  

### energy
- **trust use** = less stress and more focus  
- **trust loss** = constant defense  

### health
- **trust use** = supportive care  
- **trust loss** = isolation  

---

## a simple example

you’re on a team:
- you finish tasks on time  
- you communicate early when stuck  

now your team relies on you without hesitation.

---

## first principles summary

**trust = confidence in reliability and intent.**  
build it patiently, and it multiplies.  
break it, and everything slows.
