# microeconomics.

## start with the basic truths
i. **people face scarcity.**  
   wants are larger than available resources.

ii. **choices create trade-offs.**  
   choosing one option means giving up another.

iii. **incentives shape behavior.**  
   people respond to costs, benefits, and constraints.

iv. **prices coordinate decisions.**  
   they carry information about value and scarcity.

---

## what is microeconomics?

**microeconomics** = **the study of how individuals and small groups make choices under scarcity, and how those choices interact in markets.**

---

## microeconomics breakdown

1. **agents**  
   households, workers, firms, and consumers making decisions.

2. **constraints**  
   budgets, time, information, rules, and technology limits.

3. **incentives**  
   expected rewards and penalties that guide choices.

4. **trade-offs and opportunity cost**  
   the true cost of a choice is the next best alternative forgone.

5. **supply and demand**  
   market outcomes emerge from many decentralized choices.

6. **equilibrium and welfare**  
   where plans align, and how gains/losses are distributed.

---

## a simple example

you have $20 for lunch and transport:
- if food prices rise, you may spend less on lunch
- your budget constraint forces substitution

microeconomics explains this as **scarcity + incentives + trade-offs**.

---

## microeconomics summary

**microeconomics = choice under scarcity at the individual and market level.**
