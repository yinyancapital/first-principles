# health — first principles

## start with the basic truths
i. **the body enables everything else.**  
   without it, nothing works.

ii. **health is built daily.**  
   small habits shape long‑term outcomes.

iii. **health can recover.**  
   rest and care repair damage.

iv. **neglect compounds.**  
   small problems grow if ignored.

---

## what is health (first principles)?

**health** = **the body and mind’s capacity to function and recover.**

it relies on:

• sleep  
• movement  
• nutrition  
• recovery  

---

## health across key life resources

### time
- **health use** = routines that protect energy  
- **health loss** = sacrificing sleep and recovery  

### money
- **health use** = investing in care and prevention  
- **health loss** = avoidable costs  

### skills
- **health use** = steady practice without injury  
- **health loss** = burnout and setbacks  

### knowledge
- **health use** = learning what supports the body  
- **health loss** = ignoring warning signs  

### tools
- **health use** = supportive gear and systems  
- **health loss** = working without protection  

### relationships
- **health use** = supportive, low‑stress bonds  
- **health loss** = chronic conflict  

### networks
- **health use** = community and accountability  
- **health loss** = isolation  

### energy
- **health use** = restoring capacity  
- **health loss** = depletion  

### capital
- **health use** = long‑term earning ability  
- **health loss** = reduced capacity  

---

## a simple example

you want to feel steady all week:
- sleep on a schedule  
- move 20 minutes daily  
- eat simply and consistently  

now health supports everything else.

---

## first principles summary

**health = the capacity to function and recover.**  
protect it, and everything improves.  
ignore it, and everything weakens.
