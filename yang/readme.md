# yang — first principles

## start with the basic truths
i. **life needs movement.**  
   without action, nothing changes.

ii. **energy creates direction.**  
   force turns ideas into reality.

iii. **yang is the active side.**  
   it is drive, growth, and outward force.

iv. **yang needs balance.**  
   too much burns out; too little stalls.

---

## what is yang (first principles)?

**yang** = **the active, outward, and initiating force in a system.**

it includes:

• action  
• heat  
• momentum  
• expansion  

---

## yang across key life resources

### time
- **yang use** = decisive action  
- **yang loss** = hesitation and delay  

### money
- **yang use** = earning and investing  
- **yang loss** = idle resources  

### skills
- **yang use** = practice and performance  
- **yang loss** = underuse and drift  

### knowledge
- **yang use** = applying what you learn  
- **yang loss** = theory without action  

### tools
- **yang use** = building and producing  
- **yang loss** = unused capability  

### relationships
- **yang use** = initiative and leadership  
- **yang loss** = passivity and distance  

### networks
- **yang use** = reaching out and contributing  
- **yang loss** = waiting and missing chances  

### energy
- **yang use** = focus and drive  
- **yang loss** = scattered effort  

### health
- **yang use** = training and strengthening  
- **yang loss** = weakness from inaction  

---

## a simple example

you have an idea:
- you start small  
- you keep moving daily  

now momentum turns the idea into reality.

## first principles summary

**yang = the active force that creates movement.**  
use it well, and growth happens.  
overuse it, and balance breaks.
