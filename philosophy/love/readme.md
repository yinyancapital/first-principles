# love.

## start with the basic truths
i. **love begins with recognition.**
   to love is to truly see something as real, valuable, and worthy of care.

ii. **love moves toward union and preservation.**
   it draws attention, energy, and action toward what one does not want to neglect, damage, or lose.

iii. **love can take many forms.**
   it may be expressed toward a person, a craft, truth, life, beauty, or God.

iv. **love becomes credible through consistency.**
   what is loved is not only admired in thought, but sustained through loyalty, sacrifice, and protection.

---

## what is love?

**love** = **the durable recognition of value that organizes attention, desire, and action toward union with, care for, and the flourishing of what is held as deeply good.**
it is value made devoted.

---

## how love works

1. **recognize value**
   perceive a person, principle, or reality as deeply worthy.

2. **move toward it**
   attention and desire begin to orient around what is loved.

3. **care for it**
   love expresses itself through protection, service, discipline, or devotion.

4. **remain through difficulty**
   love persists when convenience fades and cost appears.

5. **deepen through practice**
   repeated care turns admiration into commitment and commitment into character.

---

## why love matters

- it **turns value into devotion**
- it **gives sacrifice meaning**
- it **creates endurance beyond convenience**
- it **organizes life around what matters most**

---

## a simple example

question: *why does someone keep returning to a hard craft they care about?*

first principles:
- love recognizes value beyond immediate reward
- what has deep value can justify sacrifice
- repeated devotion strengthens identity and skill

so love explains the return: **what is deeply loved is not abandoned just because it is difficult.**

---

## love summary

**love = recognition of deep value + devotion + enduring care.**
when love is real, it is not only felt — it orders life around what matters.
