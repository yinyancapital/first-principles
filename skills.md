## skills — first principles

### Start with the basic truths
1. **People can learn and improve.**  
   Skills grow through practice.
2. **Practice takes time.**  
   No skill appears instantly.
3. **Better skills create better results.**  
   They help you solve problems and build value.

---

### What are skills (first principles)?

**Skills = learned abilities that let you do things well.**

Examples:
- Writing
- Building
- Selling
- Coding
- Teaching

---

### How skills connect to time

- **Time is required** to practice.
- More practice → stronger skills.

So:
- **Time turns into skill** when used for learning.

---

### How skills connect to money and capital

- Skills can **earn money** (work, services).
- Skills can **build capital** (create tools, products, ideas).

So:
- **Skills are a form of capital** because they create value.

---

### How skills connect to Yin and Yang

- **Yin** = slow practice, patience, repetition  
- **Yang** = performance, action, delivery  

Both are needed:
- **Yin builds the skill.**
- **Yang uses the skill.**

---

### Simple example

You want to play guitar:
- **Practice 30 minutes** (Yin)  
- **Play for others** (Yang)  

Skill grows and creates value.

---

### First principles summary

**Skills = learned abilities created through time.**  
**Skills can become money and capital.**  
**Balance (Yin + Yang) = practice + performance.**
