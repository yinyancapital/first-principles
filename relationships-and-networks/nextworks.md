### Start with the basic truths
1. **People are connected in groups, not just pairs.**  
2. **Connections spread information and opportunities.**  
3. **Bigger, stronger networks create more value.**

---

### What are networks (first principles)?

**Networks = many relationships linked together.**  
They are **webs of people and connections**.

Examples:
- Communities
- Professional groups
- Online platforms
- Family trees

---

### How networks connect to relationships

- Relationships are **single links**.  
- A network is **many links together**.

So:
- **Networks are relationships at scale.**

---

### How networks connect to capital

- Networks create **access** to jobs, knowledge, and resources.
- Strong networks are **social capital**.

---

### How networks connect to knowledge and skills

- Networks help you **learn faster** (shared info).
- They help you **find mentors** or **collaborators**.

---

### How networks connect to time and money

- Networks can **save time** (faster help).
- Networks can **create money** (new deals and opportunities).

---

### How networks connect to tools

- Networks share tools and best practices.
- They spread new tools quickly.

---

### How networks connect to Yin and Yang

- **Yin** = listening, observing, staying connected  
- **Yang** = sharing, contributing, leading  

Both are needed:
- **Yin keeps the network healthy.**
- **Yang helps the network grow.**

---

### Simple example

You know 3 people.  
Each knows 3 more.

Now you are connected to many opportunities through the network.

---

### First principles summary

**Networks = many relationships working together.**  
**They are social capital at scale.**  
**Balance (Yin + Yang) = listen + contribute.**
