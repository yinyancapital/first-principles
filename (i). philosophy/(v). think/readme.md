# think.

## start with the basic truths
i. **thinking transforms input into judgment.**  
   raw signals become conclusions through mental processing.

ii. **attention is selective.**  
   what you focus on determines what you can reason about.

iii. **reasoning follows structure.**  
   assumptions, logic, and evidence shape outcomes.

iv. **thinking improves through feedback.**  
   reflection and correction produce better models over time.

---

## what is think?

**think** = **to process information deliberately in order to understand, evaluate, decide, or create.**  
it is the active operation of mind.

---

## how thinking works

1. **gather inputs**  
   collect observations, memories, and questions.

2. **frame the problem**  
   define what is being asked and why it matters.

3. **reason through possibilities**  
   compare explanations, consequences, and trade-offs.

4. **select a conclusion or action**  
   choose the best current model.

5. **review outcomes**  
   refine future thinking using results.

---

## why thinking matters

- it **turns information into understanding**  
- it **supports better choices** under uncertainty  
- it **enables learning and adaptation**  
- it **drives creativity and problem-solving**  

---

## a simple example

question: *why is writing down pros and cons useful?*

first principles:
- thoughts in memory are easy to distort  
- externalizing options clarifies structure  
- comparing trade-offs improves judgment  

so writing helps because it **makes thinking explicit and testable.**

---

## think summary

**think = attention + reasoning + evaluation → decision.**  
better structure leads to better conclusions.
